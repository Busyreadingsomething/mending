our story

Karim Galil M.D. started Mendel.ai after a family member was diagnosed with cancer. One week after she passed away, Karim found a trial that she could have qualified for, however her records were incomplete and much of the data was not accessible to hospitals or oncologists through current search methods.

The loss left Karim not only frustrated with the losing battle to cancer, but with the fragmented system that made it impossible to stay on top of the avalanche of clinical trials.

With a belief that every patient should have the opportunity to participate in potentially life saving treatments, Karim set out to combine the best in artificial intelligence, semantic search with medical language comprehension, and human intuition to reduce the disparity and to save more lives.

This journey matched Karim with Wael Salloum who, at a young age, was heavily interested in computer science. Growing up in Syria computers were non existent in schools, and Wael used to bring his desktop computer for the two mile walk to school in order to spend more time studying computers. Later he developed a vision that using a correlating and contextual architecture with machine learning, he could find hidden associations in words that could create insights from chaos and ultimately wisdom from context.

Waell’s first reiterations were slow and incomplete, however after refining the technology and providing more data, the pattern of learning developed further. Immediately Wael was able to see the results of the novel semantic search finding hidden connections of letters and words with a richness of data that would have been useless before. Joining with Karim to co-found Mendel.ai Wael has taken opportunity to utilize this technology to leave life-changing impact in healthcare and offer access to life saving treatment to more individuals.
